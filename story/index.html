Alejandra was born in the Sunshine State, among sand and surf.

The first time my mother saw me, she called me Chispa, or “little spark” in Spanish. That moniker not only stuck, but also may have had a hand in determining my fate. My existence has been anything but ordinary.

Above all else, she's always been a dreamer.

I consider myself lucky. As a kid, I looked to the outdoors as my sole source of entertainment, learning, and wisdom. Mimicking my carpenter father, I built things from random supplies and objects around my home including a fort, a sled for my dog to pull me around on, and a bug palace for the resident roly polies. Despite the sometimes gross nature of my leisure activities, they allowed me to develop an entrepreneurial spirit. I am always dreaming, doing, innovating.

She is always evolving and adapting.

My background is unique and varied for many reasons, the most important of which is that I am hungry for knowledge. Insatiable, really.

I have been everything from a legal assistant to a biologist (my degree is in Interdisciplinary Zoological Sciences) to an artist to a molecular gastronomist to a triathlete, to now, a designer. I am a researcher at heart, and am never satisfied until I get to the bottom of something. I am relentless.

Working on the web is the perfect place for me to seek nourishment, because the internet is always growing, changing, evolving – and I am too, right along with it.

She laughs fear in the face.

I bought a one-way ticket to Chicago last year after living my entire life in Florida. I used to play with alligators and venomous snakes for fun (and for work). I’ve engaged in passionate debates with clients about the shittiness of requiring binary gender choice in their forms. I guess you could say I’m practically fearless.

She is okay being the little fish in a big pond.

I think inexperience is a great thing. When it comes to building a product, I shed my ego and pretend I'm new at this; approaching things with curiosity and optimism. Workplaces that encourage growth (through both learning and teaching) are my jam.

My goal is to advocate for users and win hearts and minds for a cause I’m passionate about. You can't teach that.

who are you?

Well, if you hadn’t gleaned from the text above, I’m Alejandra Luaces-Riera. It’s okay if you can’t pronounce it on the first try. I moved to Chicago from Florida a year and a half ago. I’m currently a user experience & visual designer at Table XI, but looking for my next work-home. Check out my dribbble or twitter, or check out my resume if you are so inclined.

but table xi is great!

Yes, I agree! However, it’s getting to be a bit tough iterating on other people’s dreams, sending them off into the wild, and never hearing from them again. Table XI is a great place to work and grow (and they're my family!), but the work isn’t the right fodder for my soul.

what is this about?

Children’s stories inspire me. There's something about the innocence, the whimsical nature, the gentleness of them. I wanted to tell a story about myself modeled after a children’s story. I wanted it to feel ethereal. I wanted it to inspire a sense of wonder, while delivering relevant information about what I bring to the table, both professionally and personally. I also wanted to use this as a playground where I could test out new new front-end technologies.

why product design?

I’ve been focusing on product design for the entirety of my career in tech. I love what I do. For my restless mind, product design is a great focus. Product design encompasses all the things I love most: from writing copy that inspires curiosity, to assembling a consistent, fun visual design system, to crafting modular, graceful code. I love being able to touch every step of the process.
